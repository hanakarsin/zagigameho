William Rutos allies defied him, openly supported Aden Duales removal

June 2024 · 4 minute read

- Some Tanga Tanga MPs joined their Kieleweke counterparts in loudly endorsing Aden Duale's ouster

- Laikipia Woman Rep Catherine Waruguru sealed Duale’s fate by seconding the motion to remove him.

- Kesses MP Swarup Mishra, a Ruto ally, also said he attended the meeting to vote out the Garissa MP

- In the meeting, Uhuru said he did not have a grudge with anyone but had a problem with politicking politicians

Deputy President William Ruto watched in dismay as his close political allies openly supported the ouster of Aden Duale as the National Assembly Majority leader on Monday, June 22, it has emerged.

Shockingly for the DP, it was Laikipia Woman Representative Catherine Waruguru, who was until a fortnight ago a vocal Tanga Tanga adherent, who sealed Duale’s fate by seconding the motion to remove him during the Jubilee Parliamentary Group meeting at the Kenyatta International Convention Centre (KICC) that was presided over by President Uhuru Kenyatta.

Kesses MP Swarup Mishra, another perceived Ruto supporter, also shot up to support the motion.

In a symbolic moment that illuminated a massive rift within Tanga Tanga as well as Ruto’s waning political fortunes, a significant number of Tanga Tanga MPs turned against Duale and joined their Kieleweke wing counterparts in loudly endorsing his ouster on all the three times that Uhuru asked the Jubilee legislators whether they were in support of the removal petition.

Ruto had no option but to forlornly spectate as his trusted ally was unceremoniously ejected from the influential position he has held for the last seven years and replaced with Kipipiri MP Amos Kimunya.

Mishra would later candidly tell the media that he attended the meeting to vote out the majority leader.

“Power had got into his head and he has had no respect for members,” the MP said excitedly.

He appeared to follow in the footsteps of Waruguru, formerly an outspoken Ruto stalwart who currently has no kind words for the DP and his brigade after she recently visited former prime minister Raila Odinga at his Capitol Hill office and announced a change of loyalty to the president's camp.

She has since been firing at the DP from all cylinders, accusing him of running a parallel government to undermine the first in command.

Duale was the last man standing in the DP’s camp following a ruthless purge on other Ruto surrogates in the Senate and the National Assembly.

He somehow survived the first purge during a meeting held at State House earlier this month, a move attributed to his stature and long-standing friendship with the head of state.

Uhuru appeared to have put Duale’s fate in the hands of legislators, with Kieni MP Kanini Kega wasting no time in orchestrating a removal petition and garnering the signatures required to show the Garissa Township MP the door.

Duale did not make matters easier for himself when he appeared to demonstrate divided loyalty between Uhuru and Ruto after surviving the purge.

Tanga Tanga members were incensed that he failed to acknowledge the DP in his speech following the ouster of former Majority Whip Benjamin Washiali and his deputy Cecily Mbarire.

This left the Garissa Township legislator with nowhere to hide when Uhuru summoned this week’s PG meeting; it was clear that he was on the chopping board.

According to State House, 174 out of 179 Jubilee Party members attended the KICC meeting despite some Tanga Tanga politicians vowing to snub it.

In his address, Uhuru took the chance to make it clear that he had no grudge with all those present, including Ruto, saying the only problem was that some of them stopped working and resorted to politics after the last elections.
