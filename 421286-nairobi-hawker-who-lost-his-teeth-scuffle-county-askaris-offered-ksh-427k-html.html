Nairobi Hawker Who Lost 5 Teeth in Scuffle with County Askaris Takes KSh 427k, Drops Case

July 2024 · 2 minute read
  • Anthony Maina was assaulted by Nairobi county askaris on Monday, July 2
  • This was after the hawker reportedly refused to part with a KSh 100 bribe
  • The 25-year-old lost his five teeth and suffered a broken jaw in the scuffle

A Nairobi hawker who lost five teeth in a scuffle with county askaris has agreed to settle the matter out of court after he was offered KSh 427,000.

Anthony Maina, who sells socks along Tom Mboya Street in the capital, was offered the sum by three Nairobi county inspectorate officers identified as Humphrey Muswangi, Hassan Chege and Dennis Macharia.

“It is hereby agreed that Humphrey Muswangi, Hassan Chege and Dennis Macharia unequivocally, jointly and severally, admit and accept their personal liability over the assault on Anthony Maina on July 2 that caused him grievous harm and loss," court documents read.

Broken jaw

Business Daily reported that the 25-year-old lost his teeth on Monday, July 2, after he was assaulted by the askaris commonly referred to as 'kanjo'.

Besides losing his front teeth, the youthful man's jaw was broken.

According to The Standard, Maina would be paid KSh 300,000 for losing the five teeth (KSh 60,000 for each tooth), KSh 56,000 for the broken jaw and KSh 37,800 for food.

Maina would receive KSh to pay his rent, KSh 14,400 for clinic visits and KSh 4,000 for transport.
