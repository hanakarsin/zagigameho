Beer manufacturer KBL decries losing potential customers to betting firms

May 2024 · 2 minute read

- Statistics show betting firms and sites top among the most search queries in Kenya

- A number of youth have fallen into debts after failing to repay loans which ended in the hands of betting firms

- The government announced intention to suspend license of betting companies in July 2019

Beer maker Kenya Breweries Limited (KBL) has lamented over the shrinking of its loyal customer base in the recent times.

According to KBL managing director Jane Karuku, the loss of customers is attributed to sports betting culture and desire for quick riches among the youth who are the biggest consumers of its beverages.

Karuku noted if measures to control betting is not put in place, the country will be swamped with lazybones and layabout since the culture has been glorified as a get-rich-quick investment despite its addiction.

“Betting is a serious competitor. When you walk into any shopping centre, the proportion of betting shops is probably the highest and when youth get in, there will be a betting shop then bar,” said Karuku during a media briefing on Tuesday, April 23.

Her sentiment comes hot on the heels of a revelation by the Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i who had indicated the government will suspend all gaming and betting companies in July 2019.

A number of youth have reportedly fallen into debts with many being listed in the Credit Reference Bureau Kenya (CRB) after they failed to repay their loans which ended in the hands of betting firms.

In the annual 2019 report by Hootsuite and We Are Social, betting company Sportpesa emerged top among the most search queries followed by sports gambling related site.

Matiang’i said he would push for tough legislation aimed at not only regulating betting among the youth but also driving rogue betting firms out of business.
