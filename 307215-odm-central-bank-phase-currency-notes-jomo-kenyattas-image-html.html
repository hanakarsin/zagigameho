ODM wants Central Bank to phase out new currency notes with Jomo Kenyatta's image

August 2024 Â· 2 minute read

- ODM chairman Mbadi said the Opposition outfit fully supports newly introduced currency notes

- The MP noted any Kenyan note or coin with images of personalities contravenes the law

- He clarified the Opposition outfit would not push for recall of new notes bearing Kenyatta's image but a gradual phase out

The Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) has affirmed its support for the newly introduced currency notes but expressed concern over use of portrait of founding president Jomo Kenyatta.

Speaking to journalists on Tuesday, June 4, party chairman John Mbadi clarified the Opposition outfit did not intend to push for a recall even though it urged the Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) to find ways of phasing out those already issued notes.

According to the lawmaker, the bank regulator contravened Article 231(4) of the Constitution which states Kenyan notes and coins may only bear image that symbolise the country but shall not bear the portrait of any individual.

"The party is not calling for recall of the notes that have been issued but the CBK should think of how to phase them out. As a party, we have noticed the portrait of the first president. The truth is, it was not proper to have it on notes," he said.

The Suba South MP also asked CBK to reduce the date of devaluation of KSh 1,000 note from October 1 to August 1 considering some of the old ones shall not have expired.

Kenyans had been given up to October 1, 2019, to exchange the current KSh 1,000 notes when the new ones are expected to fully take over.

However, activist Okia Omtata has moved to court seeking to stop invalidation of the current KSh 1,000 notes as directed by CBK boss Patrick Njoroge.

The CBK, Njoroge and the Attorney General Kihara Kariuki are listed as respondents.

The new notes, just like the new coins, will serve as means of passing knowledge, conserving culture and promoting the country's uniqueness to the world.
