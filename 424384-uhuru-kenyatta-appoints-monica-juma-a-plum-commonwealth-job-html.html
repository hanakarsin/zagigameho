Uhuru Nominates CS Monica Juma for Commonwealth Secretary General Post

June 2024 · 2 minute read
  • Uhuru Kenyatta said Monica Juma had an indisputable track record exuding confidence that she would clinch the position
  • If successfully appointed, Juma would have to leave her CS job to head the organisation of 54 Britain colonies
  • Previously, Juma served as the Principal Secretary in the Ministries of Defence and Foreign Affairs

President Uhuru Kenyatta has nominated Defence Cabinet Secretary Monica Juma for the Commonwealth secretary-general post.

If successfully appointed, Juma would have to leave her CS job to head the organisation of 54 Britain colonies headquartered in London, United Kingdom.

In a statement on Monday, August 20, the head of state said Juma had an indisputable track record of strategic leadership exuding confidence that she would clinch the position.

“I have no doubt that Monica Juma will be a strong consensus candidate. Juma has an indisputable track record of strategic leadership, management, representation and knowledge of government, regional as well as multinational, and multilateral relations, international development, security, and humanitarian issues," read the statement in part.

Uhuru further lauded Juma as a diplomat par excellence, adding that she had performed well in all her other capacities.

“It is my hope that the next Commonwealth Heads of Government Summit (CHOGM) will consider this nomination favourably,” he added.

Juma previously served as the Principal Secretary in the Ministries of Defence and Foreign Affairs.

She was later promoted to be the Foreign Affairs CS before being moved to head the Defence Ministry where she is currently serving.

Manoah Esipisu appointed Commonwealth chair

In other news, Kenya's High Commissioner to the UK Manoah Esipisu was appointed to chair the Executive Committee of the Board of Governors of Commonwealth Secretariat following his appointment on July 10, 2020.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs confirmed Esipisu was voted in by the fellow board members from the other 53 Commonwealth member states.

Esipisu succeeded his colleague, Ellison Greenslade, Bahamas High Commissioner to the UK and Ireland.

The position is biennial.

The secretariat is the intergovernmental organisation that supports member countries to achieve the Commonwealth's aims of development, democracy and peace.

Prior to becoming High Commissioner, Esipisu served as spokesperson for State House in Kenya until March 2019 when he officially assumed the new office.
