The top 10 characteristics of a good business opportunity

March 2024 · 6 minute read

A challenge every aspiring entrepreneur faces is finding the right business opportunity. Should you focus on creating a new product or service based on an unmet need? Is it better to start a venture from an existing product or service? Or, is it best to go for a tried and tested business that has worked for others for many years? Sure, the market has many sources of venture opportunities for individuals. But you need to understand the characteristics of a good business opportunity before marshaling their resources.

For most people, a business opportunity arises from a need to fulfil something. For example, when you see a vacuum in the market, you have an idea of how to fill it, and you have the capability and the resources needed, then you can go ahead and start the business.

10 characteristics of a good business opportunity

Business opportunities can range from supplies, acquisitions, and franchising. However, it would be best to watch out for a few characteristics of a good business opportunity before getting started. These include:

1. Scalable

An ideal business opportunity should be scalable because you can serve it up to exponentially more people without incurring high operating costs. For a start-up to be scalable, it must focus on improving services and profitability even when the workload increases.

Efficiency and profitability can only come from the core structure of the business. Before starting, determine whether the start-up will grow in size and demand.

2. Its price point aligns with the market's

One of the many characteristics of a good business idea is price. In other words, you must consider the market and cost before starting the venture. Determine whether you can offer the service or product at a price the market can handle. Ideally, the price you want to sell your items at should attract customers and allow you to make a profit.

3. Availability of resources

Availability of resources is one of the most critical qualities of a viable business. The resources needed to start a business fall into five categories: human, financial, educational, physical, and emotional.

You can compile resources even after you have created the start-up. However, this should be the first thing to consider before proceeding. Generally, you need to have enough capital to run the business now and enough resources to help it grow in the future.

4. Solves a problem

As long as societies change and technologies advance, consumers will face different challenges or problems. Luckily, solutions for each situation will always exist. However, an issue for would-be entrepreneurs is finding these problems and matching them with the best solutions.

The best way of approaching this would be to focus on building a must-have product. Consumers have many choices on the market today. But a new entrepreneur should look for something that will solve a painful problem and grab their customer’s attention.

5. The market can accommodate the opportunity

When evaluating a good business opportunity, you should consider the market size. Conduct some research to figure out if there is a market for the business you want to set up and how big the market is.

Before you can go ahead, be sure there is a demand for it. The business opportunity may not appeal to a massive market. But understand the market, know how engaged it is, and how likely people are willing to pay for what you want to sell.

6. The business opportunity can create relationships

One of the many essential qualities of an opportunity is relationships. A good business opportunity comes with some connections and can create more relationships in the future. If the business has some relationships from the onset, it becomes easier to leverage them and help the start-up grow. From a business point of view, relationship building can help a business gain new customers, retain current ones, and manage its reputation.

7. The business matches your skills

When evaluating a business opportunity, you have to determine what you will bring to the table. If you do not have the skillset, ask yourself how you will make up for it. Your start-up may require human labour.

What skills will you look for in an employee? Will the employees be competent enough to ensure the business opportunity succeeds? Unless the business matches your skills, it will be challenging for you to run it.

8. The business will work in your location

You may have all the skills to run a business. Also, you may have found an opportunity that solves a problem, is scalable, and has a large market size. But that opportunity will not work if it is not specific to your location.

Ideally, you must figure out whether there is demand. If there is demand, it is only circulating in a particular area. Another factor to consider is the economic environment. While consumers in your area might want a solution, they may not have the time and money to get started with your start-up.

9. The timing for launching the business should be on point

Timing is everything in business and is one of the main characteristics of an attractive opportunity. Timing is even more essential than the resources required to start the business, skills, market size, and location.

You have to time the market and business location. Ideally, you have to figure out the best window for bringing your idea to the consumers. If your timing is poor, it is best to try the opportunity later.

10. The business opportunity should be measurable

A measurable business opportunity is one you can describe quantitatively. For example, you have come up with the idea of selling t-shirts online. At this point, your business opportunity is specific. But for it to be measurable, you will have to be ready to take your first order within a preferred timeframe, and you will have to sell a minimum of ten t-shirts per week, for example.
