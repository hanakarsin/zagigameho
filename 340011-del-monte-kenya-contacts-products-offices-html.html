Del Monte Kenya contacts, products, offices

May 2024 · 5 minute read

Del Monte is a household name in Kenya renowned for its amazing collections of food products. Perhaps their most popular product is their fruit juice brand that dominates the Kenyan market. Other than this, Del Monte Kenya has a considerable global presence as it exports some of its products to European markets and others. The Thika-based firm's most significant business is producing juice concentrates, cattle feed, canned solid pineapple, and mill juice sugar. However, their biggest income earner is pineapple, which they export to other countries. Their consistency as a production company in Kenya and substantial exports of pineapple has earned Kenya a spot among the top exporters around the globe.

Del Monte Kenya has a long history in Kenya's industry as they are among the first local producers of fresh products, mostly their natural juice brand. The company has a massive pineapple plantation at its headquarters in Thika, which they use as raw material for the production processes.

Del Monte Kenya history

When was Del Monte founded? The company started in 1948, which means that they are 72 years old today. Since their establishment and subsequent start of operations, they became the premier natural juice supplying company in Kenya and other regions, and today, they have a remarkable global presence.

How did they manage to make a big name from such humble beginnings, and who owns Del Monte Kenya? Perhaps a look into the firm's history would offer a functional perspective.

What is the meaning of Del Monte? There is no doubt that the name is popular in Kenya and other regions. The organization has a long history whereby it has changed ownership, diversified, and created long partnerships with other companies.

When they established the Thika-based factory, they were known as Kenya Canners, a name a bit similar to another famous brand in Kenya. After some time, the company was renamed Del Monte Royal when two South African families acquired majority control.

The South African firm took 30% control of the company. Numerous small shareholders owned 40 %. This transfer of ownership meant that the company had acquired a new name. In 2002, Del Monte Kenya altered its name once again to Cirio Del Monte Kenya Limited.

This alteration happened when Cirio Alimentare acquired a 98% stake in the firm. The move meant the firm was achieving newer capabilities, having transformed from a local to an international organization. Later, they were certified under the International Organization for Standardization in 2002, which was rectified in 2006.

Based on the firm's tremendous capability of processing pineapples on a massive scale, it has positioned Kenya as one of the lead exporters of pineapples all over the globe.

Controversy

Although the firm has positioned itself as a leader, it has been facing numerous disputes over the years. Del Monte has been receiving a lot of attention from human rights groups for a long time.

Recently they have been under intense pressure from the County Government of Murang'a that is seeking to revoke the Del Monte Kenya land lease. The main argument is that the firm is utilizing hazardous pesticides on its farm that leads to permanent damage to natural resources in the Country of Murang’a.

Del Monte occupies approximately 9,143,455 hectares of land and has over 7000 direct employees. Other than this, the company has set up learning and medical centres that serve the staff and the local population.

Del Monte Kenya has been supplying a wide range of products for many years in the country and satisfying its export market. Although certain controversies have recently marred them, the firm has had a massive impact on Kenya's economy, mostly in the local region they operate.
