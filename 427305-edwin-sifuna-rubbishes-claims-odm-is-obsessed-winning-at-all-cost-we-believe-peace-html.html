Edwin Sifuna Rubbishes Claims ODM Is Obsessed with Winning at All Cost: &quot;We Believe in Peace&quot;

August 2024 · 4 minute read
  • ODM secretary-general Edwin Sifuna has said the party is always conscious of the outcomes in an election, be it a win or loss
  • For them as a party, he said, losing is normal and never has the party been obsessed with taking the lead in a political contest
  • ODM leader Raila Odinga has on several occasions been accused by his opponents of refusing to concede defeat

The ODM party secretary-general Edwin Sifuna has stated that the opposition outfit is ever ready to embrace any election outcome, affirming that winning a political contest is not a matter of life and death for them.

He said while ODM may be determined to win in a political contest, the party will always consider the possibility of losing.

"I want to state it clear that we in the ODM party do not believe in winning election at all cost. There are things that we are not prepared to do to win. The question should be posed to other political parties, is there a line that they will not cross," said Sifuna.

He invited other outfits to embrace the same outlook of how elections are held and the possible outcomes whether or not they favour expectations.

However, he maintained that transparency and credibility would still be key to warrant concession.

In the recent past, accusations have been lavelled against the party over its perceived notoriety in contesting election results, especially in the presidential vote.

Apart from the one in 1997, Raila has disputed the results in all the elections he took part in and which were held pre and post-promulgation of the 2010 Constitution.

In 2013, the ODM chief unsuccessfully challenged President Uhuru Kenyatta's win in the Supreme Court citing malpractices and lack of transparency.

In 2017, Raila succeeded in convincing the apex court then under David Maraga who nullified the presidential results on basis of electoral irregularities.

Kenyans urge Raila to learn conceding defeat

In 2020, while celebrating the win of Ghana's president Nana Akufo Addo, Kenyans urged the former prime minister to emulate Ghana's opposition leaders who did not dispute the president's win.

Raila said he celebrated the West African country for its tradition of holding peaceful and democratic elections.

"President @NAkufoAddo congratulations for the honour and trust the people of Ghana have bestowed on you by electing you for a second term in office. We celebrate your country and your people for continuing the tradition of peaceful and democratic elections," Raila said in a tweet.

Whilst he may have sent his message in goodwill, Kenyans on social media urged him to accept defeat just like the loser in the Ghana presidential election, John Mahama, if he contests and loses in the future.

Raila: I've never lost

In his four attempts at the presidency, Raila has been insisting he only lost once since his first participation in the presidential race in 1997.
