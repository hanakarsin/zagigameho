MP Ngunjiri Wambugu claims Moses Kuria has betrayed Uhuru by revolting against the president

March 2024 Â· 3 minute read

- Ngunjiri Wambugu termed Moses Kuria's revolution against the president in Mt Kenya as imaginary

- The Nyeri Town MP argued Kuria's revolt is a betrayal to Uhuru considering he is the president's MP

- The youthful politician's statement came barely days after Mt Kenya leaders held discussion with the head of state

- However, Kuria dismissed the discussion saying they only discussed the same things they talked about in 2016

Nyeri Town MP Ngunjiri Wambugu has hit out at Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria saying he has betrayed President Uhuru Kenyatta by leading revolution against his leadership in Mt Kenya.

According to the lawmaker, Kuria was in a world of fantasy and that there is no revolution that is set to happen any time soon.

In Facebook post on Monday, November 18, the legislator said Kuria should be ashamed given it was Uhuru who helped him become MP.

"Hon Moses Kuria, MP for Gatundu South, which is the same constituency that Uhuru Kenyatta comes from and once represented as MP; hopes to be the one who will lead a revolution against Uhuru Kenyatta, that he imagines will happen in Mt Kenya.Incidentally this is the same Moses Kuria who was literally made into the MP of Gatundu South by Uhuru Kenyatta himself, on his first attempt. This attitude of raw political opportunism amongst our elected leaders in Mt Kenya is why we are where we are as a region," said Ngunjiri.

READ ALSO: Mamake Joyce Syombua asema alimuonya dhidi ya kumtembelea Mugure Nanyuki

The MP's sentiments came barely days after the Sagana meeting where the head of state held discussions with Mt Kenya leaders.

However, after the meeting Kuria said it was as good as useless because they were discussing the same issues that they talked about in 2016 and with very little progress having been made.

"Of course we have got our traditional issues, most issues to do with our agriculture and our tea. We were here in 2016 and I'm very sad that what we said then is the same thing we are saying today. You will assume there will be people from Mars who will save the problems," Kuria said.

The Gatundu South MP accused cabinet secretaries from the region of not taking the issues seriously and acting on them.

The lawmaker has been a serious critique of the president's leadership despite coming from his backyard.

At one time he bashed Uhuru accusing him of developing other regions and leaving Mt Kenya residents to languish in poverty.

Most recently, he accused the executive of using the National Assembly to rubber stamp all government proposals that have landed the country in deep financial debts.
