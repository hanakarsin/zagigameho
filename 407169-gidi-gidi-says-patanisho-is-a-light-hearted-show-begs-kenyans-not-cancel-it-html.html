Gidi Gidi Says Patanisho Is a Light-Hearted Show, Begs Kenyans Not to Cancel It

May 2024 · 3 minute read

- Gidi Gidi felt like he was next on the chopping block after Shaffie Weru and his colleagues

- The radio presenter caught wind of people's disgust regarding how he chuckles and crackles on live radio

- He pleaded with Kenyans not to cancel Patanisho and stated that laughter was the best medicine

Radio presenter Joseph Oyoo better known as Gidi Gidi is desperately pleading with Kenyans not to come for his job.

The former musician who switched his focus to journalism caught wind of guys being disheartened by how he and his co-host chuckle and crack up on radio during their popular show Patanisho.

The radio hot was termed as insensitive and a few people had threatened to add his show to their list of cancelled programmes.

In a light-hearted Facebook post, Gidi asked his fans not to pass any harsh judgement against him.

The journalist added that Patanisho was a free and fun icebreaker show with light moments which involve making partners reunite.

To him, his segment bears no ill will or negativity.

“We do not have any vendetta against any gender. Laughter is medicine and can make quarrelling couples start talking. Please do not switch us off,” he asked social media users.

As earlier reported by TUKO.co.ke, Radio Africa Group-owned Homeboyz Radio terminated the services of its three presenters who were implicated in a victim-shaming scandal.

The station, in an official statement, announced it had dismissed Shaffie Weru, Joseph Munoru alias Dj Joe Mfalme and Neville Muysa following their remarks on Thursday, March 25, during their breakfast show dubbed the 'Lift Off'.

The remarks were deemed to endorse and promote gender violence.

Terming the decision as the "right thing to do", the media house stated it had fired the trio following a comprehensive assessment of their alleged comments that appeared to be vouching for gender-based abuse.

"The termination follows a comprehensive review of the comments made on the station on Thursday morning and their subsequent suspension from employment. Our partners and listeners will not be surprised by this action to terminate their services as it is the right thing to do," read the statement in part.
