Lupita Nyongo family

The beautiful dark-skinned celebrity comes from the Luo tribe in Kenya. Both her parents are from the Luo tribe.

Her father is the incumbent governor of Kisumu, Professor Anyang Nyong'o, and her mother is Dorothy Nyong'o. She has a brother and a sister namely, Peter Nyong'o and Esperenza Nyong'o respectively.

Lupita is the daughter to Peter Anyang' Nyong'o - father, and Dorothy Ogada Buya - mother. She was born in Mexico. This was when her parents sought political refuge, in 1980, after a political crisis emerged in Kenya. She was born in 1983 in Mexico and named Lupita to symbolize the condition and environment of where she was born. This is in line with the Luo child naming tradition, which her parents subscribe to.

After her birth, her family spent a few months in Mexico and later relocated to Nairobi, Kenya. When she was 16, her parents sent her to Mexico so that she could learn Spanish. In case you were wondering the number of languages Lupita Nyong'o speaks, please stop because they are three - English, Kiswahili, and Spanish.

When did her love for acting begin

Lupita Nyong'o parents enjoyed watching plays and would often take the family to the theatre to watch during their family get together. Children would also perform plays for everyone. This was when a heart for acting grew in the little Ms. Nyong'o. To fuel her passion, Lupita took part in school plays while at Rusinga International School in Kenya.

Lupita Nyong'o Education

From Rusinga International School, Lupita Joined St. Mary's School in Nairobi, where she graduated with an IB Diploma.

She later flew out of Kenya and into the US to pursue her education at the Hampshire College where she graduated with a degree in film and theatre studies.

Lupita then joined the Yale School of Drama to enhance her skills. She graduated with a Masters in Fine Arts (MFA)

Professional acting

She started off her acting career with a role in Romeo and Juliet - produced by the Nairobi-based repertory company Phoenix Players. She also played a role in another play dubbed The Razzle and There Goes The Bride.

After graduating from Hampshire College, Lupita began working as part of the production crew for a number of films that include, Where God Left His Shoes, The Namesake, and The Constant Gardner.

She was then inspired to try acting by Ralph Fiennes when her time at the production crew came to an end. And in 2008, she got a role in the East River - a short film.

She later returned home, Kenya, and featured in Shuga - A UNICEF/MTV Base drama geared to raise awareness on HIV prevention. The following year, Lupita scripted, directed and produced 'In My Genesis' - a documentary that told the story of discriminated Kenyan Albino population. To her surprise, the documentary won First Prize at the 2008 Five College Film Festival.

When she Joined Yale, Lupita got involved in a number of projects including Taming of The Shrew, Uncle Vanya, and The Winter's Tale, among others. Her performance during her stay in the school earned her the Herschel Williams Prize in 2011-2012 for acting students with outstanding abilities.
