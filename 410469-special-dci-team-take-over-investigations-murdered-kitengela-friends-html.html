Special DCI Team Takes Over Investigations Into Murder of Kitengela Friends

June 2024 · 3 minute read

- The four Kitengela friends were all in the car hire business and it was suspected that a deal gone sour could have led to their disappearance

- The four buddies were abducted shortly after leaving a restaurant in Kitengela, Kajiado county

- The remains of Elly Obuong and Benjamin Imbai have since been positively identified

- Jack Ochieng's family, however, said the body claimed to be his discovered in Mathioya was not that of their son

A special Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) homicide team has been formed to investigate the murder of three Kitengela friends who were found dead on Thursday, April 29, within a 30-km radius.

The team was formed under the orders of Inspector General of Police Hillary Mutyambai who called for thorough investigations into the mysterious killings of Elly Obuong, Benjamin Imbai, and Jack Ochieng'.

The police are yet to establish the whereabouts of Brian Odour, a fourth friend who had spent time with the deceased before they were abducted and killed.

The four men were all in the car hire business and it was suspected that a deal gone sour could have led to their disappearance.

Speaking on Friday, April 30, at a cadets event at the National Police College in Kiganjo, the IG tasked the DCI team to speedily launch investigations into the sad incident and report to him.

"This morning, I directed the DCI homicide team to investigate this matter in order to get to the bottom of it. The team has already begun its work,” Mutyambai stated.

Three bodies found

Obuong's body was the first to be spotted by sand harvesters near Mukungai River along Murang'a Road on Tuesday, April 20, and was taken to Murang'a morgue.

The deceased's body was later identified by his brother Michael Amollo on Thursday, April 29, who said that the body had no visible injuries.

Imbai's body on the other hand was found in a Thika mortuary where it had been lying since April 20.

Police officers said his body was taken to the mortuary, a day after he and the three others disappeared.

The remains of Jack Ochieng meanwhile, were found in River Mathioya, just a few meters from where Elijah Obuong's body was found.

Jack's family, however, have said the body claimed to be his discovered in Mathioya was not that of their son.
