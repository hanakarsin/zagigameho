Deputy President William Ruto has criticised the state following the arrest of Mathira MP Rigathi Gachagua, terming it as politically instigated.

Detectives probing Gachagua over some KSh 12 billion fraudulent tenders

His arrest is connected with a probe into how some 49 companies he is linked with benefited from tenders worth KSh 12 billion, which the state argues was fraudulently acquired.

But the second in command said the arrests of his close confidants would come to an end soon.

Taking to his official Twitter handle on the evening of Friday, July 23, Ruto said the allegations were trumped up.

The deputy president also claimed that his distractors were using state machinery to intimidate political leaders who subscribe to his ideologies.

''My bro Gachagua, I know you are strong because we knew these politically instigated trumped-up charges were coming. However, this persecution on account of our friendship and political beliefs will come to an end. They have connections, networks, pedigree and godfathers. But we have God our Father,'' Ruto said.

Lawmaker was arrested from his Nyeri home

Detectives from the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) picked Gachagua from his Sagana home in Nyeri county in the early hours of Friday, July 23.

Immediately, Gachagua took to his Facebook page and said he was being accused of money laundering related charges.

He would later post on the Facebook page saying he had arrived safely at the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) headquarters on Kiambu Road.

In the post, Gachagua attached newspaper copies implicating the first family in graft-related claims.

Gachagua, who is spending the week in the cells, said that he was still waiting to be informed about the charges that led to his arrest.

''We arrived safely at DCI. We have been waiting for the charges. I am told the file is with DCI Kinoti. I trust he also has the following files for the first family,'' Gachagua said.

But according to a report by NTV Kenya, the charge sheet indicates that Gachagua is to be charged with fraudulently obtaining KSh 27 million from the Nyeri county government.

''Conspiracy to defraud Nyeri county KSh 6 million. Money laundering involving some KSh 112 million, money laundering involving some KSh 7 million and conflict of interest regarding some KSh 800,000,'' the charge sheet reads in part.

Protests broke out in Karatina after Gachagua's arrest

Riots broke out in Karatina town in the wake of his arrest.

The protestors barricaded the busy Nyeri-Nairobi highway setting tyres ablaze, causing a traffic snarl-up.

Police had to use teargas canisters to disperse the group.

Uasin Gishu woman rep Gladys Shollei, one of Gachagua's lawyers, said that the vocal legislator had been denied a free bond.

''We have been told that he will appear on Monday, July 26 in court. The detectives said that they have instructions that he must spend the weekend in the cells,'' said Shollei.

Those in Ruto's corner termed his arrest as state prosecution, Rigathi being a staunch Ruto's supporter.
