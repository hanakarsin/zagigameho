Famous Shakespeare quotes about life and love

July 2024 · 6 minute read

The world has seen some of the best people live through its times. While we may not have been fortunate to meet or experience them first hand, some of the best gifts they left behind are in the form of sayings and great quotes. These are what Shakespeare quotes are all about. The great artist and thinker had a thing or two to say about every aspect of life as captured in some of his great quotes.

As a creative, Shakespeare experienced life through his own eyes. It was special and spectacular. It is this uniqueness that makes his sayings relevant even today. Whether through his plays or other literary works, it is easy to see that the man was full of wisdom. Check out the following quotes.

    Some of the most famous Shakespeare quotes are on the subject of love. It seems as though the best Shakespeare quotes come loaded with emotions that trigger some of the deepest feelings in people. Here are a few examples of Shakespeare quotes about love.

  • Love is a smoke made by fumes of sighs.
  • ‘There’s beggary in love that can be reckoned’ - Antony & Cleopatra – Act 1, Scene 1
  • I would not wish any companion in the world but you.
  • Stars hide your fires, let not light see my black and deep desires.
  • Love goes by haps; Some Cupid kills with arrows, some with traps’ - Much Ado About Nothing – Act 3, Scene 2
  • Love cannot be found where it does not exist, nor can it be hidden where it truly exists.
  • Love is like a child, That longs for everything it can come by’ - The Two Gentlemen of Verona – Act 3, Scene 1
  • The course of true love never did run smooth.
  • Doubt thou the stars are fire, doubt that the sun doth move, doubt truth to be a liar but never doubt that I love.
  • Cupid is a knavish lad, thus to make females mad - A Midsummer Night’s Dream – Act 3, Scene 3
  • When I saw you I fell in love and you smiled because you knew.
  • Let her sleep for when she wakes she will move mountains.
  • Whoever loved that loved not at first sight?’ - As You Like It – Act 3, Scene 5
  • Love is too young to know what conscience is.
  • Love looks not with the eyes but with the mind and therefore is winged cupid painted blind.
  • Though she be but little, she is fierce.
  • ‘I love you more than words can wield the matter, Dearer than eyesight, space and liberty’ - King Lear – Act 1, scene 1
